Project 2: Wires

Description

Continuing from Project 1, in this project we will be building a structure for searching and posting an item on Craigslist Vancouver.

You will be required to build out the following:

  • an overview of your section with a listing of subsections
  • a navigation stucture that allows jumping to different sections and viewing listings in each section with a variety of navigation techniques
  • an item detail section
  • posting an item to your section

Even though you will not necessarily build out all of these sections, your navigation for the website must presume that the user will need to be able to:

  • get a clear overview of available sections subsections and items
  • be able to understand how to search for an item
  • can complete the posting of an item

Any links that do not have accompanying pages should link back to the home-page.

Due

Before your Lab 6.

Worth

20% of your final grade.

Instructions

There are a number of weeks in which to complete this project, please pay attention to what is required from you individually as your process grade will be influenced by it.

Part 1

  1. As a group build out one main sitemap illustrating the entirety of the site. It should provide us with a clear understanding of:

    • hierarchy of pages
    • linking between pages
    • relationship and association of pages

  2. As a group, fill out the Project 2 contract available on Canvas.

  3. Individually, create vector based rendered wireframes (using draw.io) illustrating the hierarchy of each of the pages on your sitemap. There will be some common pages, only create one wireframe for these. The wireframes should:

    Example: only create 1 wireframe page to represent the subsections: technology, outdoors, kitchen, etc... (they will all look the same)

    • use a grid structure
    • help us see general hierarchy on the pages
    • what elements of the structure will be common
    • what elements of the structure will change

Part 1 - Due in Lab 04

  • 1 set of VECTOR (Draw.io/Axure) wireframes for all pages (per individual)
  • 1 sitemap, printed (per group)
  • 1 contract, printed (per group)

Part 2

  1. Individually, generate a flowchart illustrating the process of completing one of posting an item. Ensure that your flowchart SHOWS THE FOLLOWING:

    • ORDER of user ACTIONS
    • WHAT DECISIONS the user can make and their outcomes
    • WHAT user INPUT is needed, and WHEN
    • HOW they will complete the task

  2. Individually, generate a set of higher-fidelity VECTOR wireframes illustrating how the different screens may look for the flow-chart you just created. It is required that these wireframes:

    • use a grid structure
    • illustrate key interaction points and actual text for headings and labels
    • demonstrate a clear hierarchy of content

Part 2 - Due in Lab 5

  • 1 flowchart, printed (per individual)
  • 1 set of screen wireframes (related to the flowchart), PDF Submission on Canvas (per individual)

Part 3

  1. As a group create 1 finalized sitemap and flowchart (PDF) based on the feedback you have received.

  2. As a group create 2 sets of interactive wireframes using IMG MAP HTML tags and then submitted in a single folder ZIPPED up. One set must be for a desktop display (1280px wide), while the other set must be for a smartphone display (480px wide). Note the following rules to avoid grade deductions:

    • use a clear grid structure
    • only build the pages listed at the top of the brief
    • no branded elements are to be included (placeholders only)
    • only shades of grey, one orange, one blue, black and white are permitted for colours
    • only different weights of Arial is permitted
    • different pages should link to one-another appropriately

  3. As a group prepare a 2 minute presentation that illustrates how your site will collapse effectively, and help the user complete the chosen use case.

  4. Submit all materials to Canvas, and bring your prepared presentation to lab.

Final Delivery

Due to Canvas before your Lab 06. All items should be clearly labelled with your names and please remember that professionalism in your submission is important. Grades will be deducted for submissions lacking in required information or quality.

  • 1 finalized sitemap and flowchart for the chosen activities (PDF)
  • 2 final set of wireframes, for desktop and mobile (generated to HTML, submitted in ZIP)
  • 1 set of presentation slides, presented in lab
