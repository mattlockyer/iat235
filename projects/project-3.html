Project 3: Web

Description

Continuing from Project 2 in this project a responsive web coded demo of your redesigned Craigslist section

You will be required to build out the following:

  • listings of items
  • navigation to different views and subsections
  • posting an item

Even though your website might not functionally perform all these actions, your final submission must allow the user to experience:

  • a clear overview of available items, views, subsections and navigation
  • be able to post an item and preview their post

Any links that do not have accompanying pages should link back to your section and a default view.

Due

Before your Lab 12.

Worth

25% of your final grade

Instructions

There are a number of weeks in which to complete this project, please pay attention to what is required from you individually as your process grade will be influenced by it.

This project differs in that we will be focusing on code and content for the first few weeks and then design and usability in the later weeks.

Week 7 (Code week)

  1. As a group, build out all of your HTML pages. Once done building them out, ensure they validate (this will be checked in the lab next week). Use real content.

  2. Individually, style these pages using CSS. Do not worry about responsive layouts and fine detail positioning of elements, though ensure the following:
    • Basic page structure (nav, layout, main)
    • Initial ideas for typography, borders, hierarchy, color
    • There is only one CSS file for all pages
    • You have a clean file and folder structure
    • That your CSS validates (will be checked in lab)
  3. Individually, identify what content is needed. This list should include:
    • what kinds of photos or images might be necessary
    • what icons might be used
    • any maps or information visuals that might be required
  4. Individually, from your list of content compile the image content you require as needed (some options listed below). Try use realistice photos that would represent items correctly.
    • The Noun Project (Icons)
    • Creative Commons Search
    • Google Image Search

Week 7 deliverables (due in your Week 8 lab):

  • 1 link to a set of completed and validated HTML pages (per group, hosted)
  • 1 list of content and images (per individual)
  • 1 link to individually created, CSS styled pages (per individual, hosted)

Week 8 (Code week)

  1. As a group, consolidate your work including HTML, CSS stylings and content.

  2. Individually, submit at least one project-related question to Canvas before the end of lab.

  3. Individually, with your subset of pages apply a responsive grid structure. Ensure the following:

    • HTML and CSS validates
    • your grids are defined in a grid.css file
    • grids are responsive; they collapse on smaller devices or displays

Week 8 deliverables (due in your Week 9 lab):

  • 1 link to a consolidated set of HTML, CSS and content (per group, hosted)
  • 1 link to a subset of responsive pages (per individual, hosted)

Week 9 (Design week)

  1. As a group, conslidate your pages into one set. Ensure that:
    • HTML and CSS validates
    • You have a clean file and folder structure
    • Appropriate file naming (it make sense)
    • The website is functioning as expected on different devices
    • We can begin testing and talking about the design of the pages

Week 9 deliverables (due in your Week 10 lab):

  • 1 link to completed responsive website (per group, hosted)

Week 10 (Design week)

  1. Based on crits in the labs revise your website as a group, focus on:
    • Creating a consistent experience between devices
    • Ensure the user knows how to navigate the hierarchy of information
    • Effective use of information (visual, textual, interactive)
    • Creating a positive experience for the user
    • Clarifying interaction and navigation user-wise

Week 10 deliverables (due in your Week 11 lab):

  • 1 link to a completed and revised responsive website (per group, hosted)

Week 12: Final Delivery

Final deliverables are due to Canvas before Lab 13. All items should be clearly labelled with your names and please remember that professionalism in your submission is important. Grades will be deducted for submissions lacking in required information or quality.

As a group prepare a presentation which clearly describes how the design is successful at helping users find items and post an item. Make sure to:

  • Provide clear examples of how you have fulfilled your requirements
  • Be as professional as possible in your presentations
  • Keep the presentation under 5 minutes
  • 1 final responsive HTML+CSS website (on your server space)
  • 1 set of presentation slides with URL to final project (PDF)
